Parameteric Representation of a Love Star

There are many representations of a love star. The most common one is \[ \begin{aligned} x(t)&= 16 \sin{t}^{3} \\ y(t)&= -13 \cos{t} +5\cos{2t}+2\cos{3t} +\cos{4t} \end{aligned} \] ## Perturbation

The love star has some diffusion-like points around it. So we need to make some perturbation.

In Probability theory we have the following conclusions:

Assume X is a continous random variables with p.d.f \(f_X(x)\) and \(Y = g(X)\) is a bijection - $f_Y(y)=f_X(g^{-1}(y)) $

Assume X,Y are two continous random variables with jointly probability density function \(f_{X,Y}(x,y)\), and U,V are transformation of X,Y. Then - \(f_{U,V}(u,v) = f_{X,Y}(x,y)\frac{1}{\left\vert \det \right\vert }\), where det = det \(\frac{\partial (U,V)}{\partial (X,Y)}\)

Assume X is a uniform distribution on \([0,1]\), then - \(Y=\ln{X}\), \(f_Y(y)=e^y ,y \leqslant 0\) - \(Y=e^X, f_Y(y) = \frac{1}{y}, 0\leqslant y \leqslant e\)

So if we choose a exponential distribution, we will use \(Y= \ln{X}\)
